## Root cause

The strategy's qty formula (per `SweepReclaimConfig.risk_per_trade_pct`):

```
qty = risk_per_trade_pct Ã— equity / (stop_distance_ticks Ã— $-per-tick)
```

This produces a smooth float. Wider stops produce smaller qty, AND
wider stops have higher win rates (price has more room). So:

- **Wider stop**: smaller qty (e.g. 0.5) AND wins more often
- **Tighter stop**: larger qty (e.g. 1.0) AND loses more often

In R-multiple terms (dimensionless), the strategy looks profitable
because the per-trade R-edge is positive.

In USD terms, the asymmetric sizing means **losing trades risk 2Ã— the
dollar amount of winning trades**, which flips the aggregate sign
from R-positive to USD-negative.

---

## Compounding problem: qty=0.5 is invalid for the broker

A real IBKR broker will REJECT a qty=0.5 MES order. Micro futures
contracts trade in integer increments only. The paper sim accepts the
fractional value and computes a fractional fill â€” which is how this
bug stayed hidden. It works in simulation but fails in live execution.

## Proposed fixes (any one suffices)

### Fix A â€” Integer-only qty (minimum-blast-radius)

```python
qty = max(1, math.floor(risk_per_trade_pct * equity / (stop_distance_ticks * dollar_per_tick)))
```

Round DOWN to integer; minimum of 1. This eliminates fractional
sizing. Downside: very small accounts can't size below 1 contract.

### Fix B â€” Veto fractional-qty signals

If the computed qty is < 1, refuse the trade entirely. The wave-25
risk gate already does this for some scenarios; extending to qty < 1
is a one-line change.

### Fix C â€” Constant-USD risk (recommended)

Instead of constant-R risk-per-trade, target a constant USD risk.
Pick qty FIRST (always 1 for micro futures eval) and size the stop
to fit:

```python
qty = 1
stop_distance_ticks = max(min_stop_ticks, risk_per_trade_usd / dollar_per_tick)
```

Guarantees losses are bounded equally regardless of setup. Trade-off
is losing the qty-scaling lever for very high-conviction setups.
